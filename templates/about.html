MOMA With Modern Art for Future!

Explore Art with Modern Vibes!

United States, New York

Founded in 1929, the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in downtown Manhattan was the first museum dedicated to the modern era. Today, MoMA's rich and diverse collection offers a comprehensive overview of modern and contemporary art, ranging from innovative European paintings and sculptures of the 1880s to contemporary film, design, and performance art. From its initial donation of eight prints and one drawing, the collection has grown to include more than 150,000 paintings, sculptures, drawings, prints, photographs, architectural models and drawings, and design objects; approximately 22,000 films and four million film stills; and, in its Library and Archives, more than 300,000 books, artist books, and magazines, as well as extensive individual archives on more than 70,000 artists. Highlights of the collection include Claude Monet's Water Lilies, Vincent van Gogh's Starry Night, and Pablo Picasso's Les Demoiselles d'Avignon, along with recent works by Andy Warhol, Elizabeth Murray, Cindy Sherman, and many others.
